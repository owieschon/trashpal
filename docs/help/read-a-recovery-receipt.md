# Read a recovery receipt

Use this page to understand what TrashPal records after a recovery operation changes state.

## What a receipt records

A recovery receipt ties the case, the reviewed recovery, the approval, the operation, and the outcome information together. It helps you answer practical questions later:

- Which case did this work address?
- What recovery did the dispatcher approve?
- Which records supported that decision at the time?
- What did the dispatch system report?
- What outcome information arrived after the request?

## What a receipt does not prove

A receipt is an audit record, not evidence that the collection was completed. A driver report or supporting attachment can show that field information arrived, but it does not by itself confirm the customer outcome.

Read the receipt status before closing a case. If customer confirmation, a dispute, or a reopen is still outstanding, keep that distinction visible instead of treating the work as settled.

## Use it for follow-up

Use the receipt when a customer asks what happened, when an operation must be checked again, or when a team needs to understand why a recovery was approved. It is the record of the decision and its subsequent evidence, not a substitute for an operating policy.
